# 什么是论证

Either the [[butler]] or the gardener did it.​
	The [[butler]] didn’t do it.
    ∴The gardener did it.

^8ac583
*The three dots on the third line of the argument are read 'therefore'.*

They indicate that the final sentence expresses the **conclusion** of the argument. The two sentences before that are the [[premise|premises]] of the argument.
上述示例最后一句话表示论证的结论,一二句话表示的是**前提** ^b741c2


A **single sentence** can contain a **complete argument**.
**一个句子**也可以表达一个**完整的论点**

![[alibi#^ffbc60]]

Many arguments start with premises, and end with a conclusion, but not all of them.
许多论点从前提开始结论结尾, 但并不全是.
即有些论点**先给出结论再进行验证.**

![[after all#^4dccdd]]

Equally, it might have been presented with the conclusion in the middle.
同理, 他也可以用于**中间**
	The butler didn’t do it. Accordingly, it was the gardener, given that it was either the gardener or the butler.

First thing to do is to separate out the conclusion from the premises. As a guide, these words are often used to indicate an argument’s conclusion.
首先要**分开前提和结论**, 由此有一些词可供使用.

so, therefore, hence, thus, accordingly, consequently
(**因此**)
 They are sometimes called **conclusion [[indicator]] words**.
 也叫做**结论标志词**
# 句子

 In logic, we are only interested in sentences that can figure as a premise or conclusion of an argument.
 逻辑上,对于论证只采用前提和结论两种表述方式,即为**陈述句**.
